What is this thing

Somewhere behind this desktop sits a machine learning model that took one look at thousands of laptops and decided it understood the market better than a Best Buy salesperson. You give it specs. It gives you a number. That number is usually followed by a long, quiet sigh.

The model was trained on CleanedData.csv, a spreadsheet full of laptops that existed, had prices, and did not ask to be turned into a regression problem. We respect their sacrifice.

How the magic works

You fill out a form describing a laptop that is, statistically speaking, nicer than what you currently own. The form gets sent to a Flask backend, where an over-engineered pipeline of preprocessing, encoding, and one very confident regression model decides what your dream machine would actually cost. Spoiler: more than you hoped.

A note on your budget

We don't know your budget. We don't need to. Statistically, the laptop you're about to spec out costs more than you were planning to spend, because that's just how laptops work. The model isn't judging you. The model's architecture is judging you. There's a difference, apparently.

Disclaimer: predictions are generated by a model, not a psychic, a banker, or your mother, all of whom would probably also tell you this laptop is too expensive.
